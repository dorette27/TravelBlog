People

The population of Nepal comprises of about a 101 ethnic groups speaking over 92 languages. The distinction in caste and ethnicity is understood more easily with a view of customary layout of the population.

Language

Though, there exist over 92 dialects, the language of unification is the national language, Nepali. Nepali is the official language of the state, spoken and understood by majority of the population. Multiple ethnic groups have their own mother tongues.

Dress

Nepal is the country with diversity of culture and tradition. The major national dress for men is Dhaka topi and Daura Surwal, and Guniyo Cholo for female. But you can see more beautiful cultural costums, and language according to the ethnic groups.

Festival

Nepal is not only the land of mountains; it is also the land of festivals. There are more than 50 festivals celebrated in Nepal every year. Each ethnic group has their own festivals. However, dashain is regarded as the biggest festival in Nepal.

Food

Dal-bhat-tarkari is eaten throughout Nepal. Dal is a soup made of lentils and spices. This is served over boiled grain, usually rice, some vegetables and pickles. Though, Dhindo is a traditional food of Nepal, there are numerous other foods which people have in their every day life.

Geography

Nepal is located in South Asia between China in the north and India in the south, east and west. The country can be divided into three main geographical regions: Himalayan region, mid hill region and Terai region.

Mountains

Nepal has the highest mountain range in the world. Eight of the fourteen highest peak (above 8000sq km) of the world are located in the country. The highest mountain in the world, Mount Everest is also in Nepal.

Lakes

Nepal is the second richest country in water resources. There are numerous lakes and tanks of glacial and tectonic origin in Nepal. Nepal is famous for its high altitude fresh water lakes of varying size. This is the highest lake of Nepal, Tilicho Lake stands at 4919m from mean sea level.

Historical Places

The structures of Kathmandu, Bhaktapur and Patan’s medieval Durbar Square are a magical place to watch traditional city life in Nepal. The 1500-year-old Licchavi statues of Changu Narayan Temple rank it as a treasurehouse of Himalayan art. Similarly, the birth place of Buddha, Lumbini is one of the finest attractions for people in the Nepal.

National Parks

Nepal serves as one of the best collection of wild life on the planet - From exotic Lophophorus to the elusive Royal Bengali Tiger, and from the endangered One-Horned Rhino to exotic Snow leopard. It has 9 national parks where you can find the largest collection of birds and butterflies in the world. Even in an small area of less than 147,181 sq. km, Nepal have a wide variety of wild floras and faunas that is incomparable with any other geographic Nation of the world.

Temples

Nepal’s Hindu temples are masterworks in oiled brick, stone and carved wood. Awe-inspiring statuary, incredibly ornate toranas (lintels) and erotic carvings still inspire the desired amount of wonder.

Stupas

Nepal has a rich Buddhist culture and temples which were nurtured over a long time. There are said to be over 1,200 Buddhist temples in Nepal, some going back as far as 2,000 years. As well as in the Kathmandu Valley, monasteries can be found in many of the mountain districts that border onto Tibet to the north and India to the east.
